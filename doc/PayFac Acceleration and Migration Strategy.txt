3. Migration Strategy
Clarity on IC and PSN migration strategies and cohorts is still emerging. Defining these plans quickly is critical to accelerate Beta and GA. Without a clear, repeatable migration model for PSN and IC billers,
 momentum stalls and scale cannot be achieved.
Why this matters:
Migration is the most visible proof point to the board and Chase that our PayFac model works.
Repeatability in migrations demonstrates that our onboarding, billing, funding, and compliance levers are functioning consistently.
Without clear cohorts and eligibility rules, every migration looks like a one-off, delaying momentum.
PSN:
Define cohorts based on implementation complexity, operational readiness, and biller profile (size, revenue impact, vertical risk).
Resolve data portability (tokens, IDs) and finalize POS/Bluefin strategy.
Establish repeatable batch tooling and cutover playbooks.
Decide PwC role (advisory vs. execution) and assign clear ownership.
IC:
Set clear goals and expectations for IC migration
 to maintain organizational focus and ensure progress can be measured and communicated.
Select high-revenue, lower-complexity billers early to show meaningful impact quickly.
Prioritize closing product disparity gaps (statements, billing detail, dispute workflows) so migrations are consistent.
Defer complex, one-off migrations until after repeatable process is proven.
Concrete cohort criteria:
Revenue impact:
 Prioritize billers that represent meaningful revenue uplift.
Complexity:
 Start with cohorts that avoid complex edge cases â€” e.g., no POS device migrations, no custom or mixed pricing models, no multiâ€‘TIN superâ€‘billers, and no unusual funding/statement requirements. These more complex migrations can be deferred until after a repeatable baseline process is proven.
Risk:

Product parity:
 Only migrate billers whose needs are supported by Beta features (e.g., billing visibility, chargeback intake).
Output:
 Cohort eligibility rules, migration tooling, and cutover playbooks = a repeatable migration playbook that demonstrates acceleration by showing PSN completed and IC momentum by EOY.
