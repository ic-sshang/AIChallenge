Roles & Permissions
What is the problem that needs solving?
The primary issue is the time-consuming and cumbersome process of manually setting and managing permissions for each user in the Biller Portal. Billers also face difficulties in limiting and tracking staff actions due to the lack of a granular permission system.
Currently, permissions are often too broadly granted or denied, leading to inefficiencies and potential security risks. Additionally, there is no efficient way to manage changes in permissions for multiple users, which is particularly challenging for large organizations with numerous staff members.
What is the preferred solution to this problem?
Implement a granular permission system with predefined user roles (Super Admin, Admin, CSR, Finance, IT) and customizable permissions. This system will provide an audit trail of permission changes, streamline the permission-setting process, and enhance management of changes in permissions.
Who is the target audience for this solution?
The primary target audience includes Biller Administrators, Customer Service Representatives, Finance and Compliance Teams, and Support Teams within billing organizations.
What are the costs associated with this solution?
Development time.
What are the primary features of this solution?
Predefined user roles with customizable permissions.
Ability for Admins to create, edit, and delete roles.
Audit trail for tracking changes in permissions.
Role-based access control for efficient permission management.
User-friendly interface in the Biller Portal for managing roles and permissions.
Are there revenue or cost savings opportunities for this?
YES (Streamlining permission management could reduce operational costs and improve efficiency during onboarding/implementation.)
Does this solution alter our competitive positioning?
YES (Improving permission management could enhance our product's appeal and competitive edge in the market.)
How do we measure success on this project?
Reduction in permission-related support tickets.
Positive feedback from early access group (NFG, City of Clarksville Gas & Water Department, etc.).
Successful deployment by the General Availability release this year.
User satisfaction and ease of use in permission management.
Southern Farm Permissions by Invoice Type
What is the problem that needs solving?
The main problem is the need for a centralized system to handle insurance and membership/PAC contributions for Southern Farm Bureau, which operates across six states. This system must allow for separate yet integrated handling of insurance and membership payments, with specific data visibility restrictions for federations, insurance administrators, and certain executives.
What is the preferred solution to this problem?
The preferred solution is to transition all federations and insurance payments for Southern Farm Bureau to Invoice Cloud. The system will have features to restrict data visibility and allow payers to make separate or combined payments.
Who is the target audience for this solution?
The target audience includes federations and insurance administrators across Arkansas, Colorado, Florida, Louisiana, Mississippi, and South Carolina, as well as executives at the Southern Farm Bureau who require access to comprehensive data.
What are the costs associated with this solution?
Development time.
What are the primary features of this solution?
Restriction of data visibility for federations and insurance administrators to prevent access to other states' data/payments.
A payment system allowing payers to make membership and insurance payments separately or together via a shopping cart.
Access for certain executives at Southern Farm to view all data.
Are there revenue or cost savings opportunities for this?
YES
Does this solution alter our competitive positioning?
YES
How do we measure success on this project?
Success can be measured by the smooth integration of federations and insurance payments onto Invoice Cloud, adherence to data visibility restrictions, user satisfaction, and efficiency in handling payments.

Open Billing IVR
What is the problem that needs solving?
Customers are not aware of their outstanding balance after submitting a payment via the IVR system, leading to confusion and incomplete transactions.
What is the preferred solution to this problem?
Implementing a new IVR solution that includes an additional API call to check the updated outstanding balance post-payment. If the balance is not zero, the IVR will provide the remaining balance and offer an option to make another payment. If the balance is zero, the call flow remains unchanged.
Who is the target audience for this solution?
The target audience includes customers using the IVR system for our billers that utilize an open billing system.
What are the costs associated with this solution?
Development time.
What are the primary features of this solution?
Real-time balance update post-payment via IVR.
Option to make additional payments if the balance is not zero.
Seamless integration into existing call flow without requiring customers to re-enter account information.
Are there revenue or cost savings opportunities for this?
YES (This solution can potentially reduce customer service calls and improve payment completion rates.)
Does this solution alter our competitive positioning?
YES (It enhances customer experience and may provide a competitive edge in terms of customer service and payment processing efficiency.)
How do we measure success on this project?
Success can be measured by a reduction in customer service calls related to balance inquiries, an increase in completed transactions, and positive feedback from customers regarding the new system.

Multi-Factor Authentication
What is the 
problem
 that needs solving?
The biller portal currently lacks modern-day security measures, rendering it vulnerable to unauthorized access and potential data breaches. Additionally, the current system does not fully meet PCI DSS 4.0 standards, which is critical for ensuring the security of credit card transactions and maintaining customer requirements, both pre- and post-sales.
What is the preferred solution to this problem?
Implementing Multi-factor Authentication (MFA) for the biller portal to enhance security and ensure compliance with PCI DSS 4.0. This solution will require users to provide two or more verification factors to access their accounts, thereby adding an extra layer of protection against unauthorized access and ensuring the security of customer transactions.
Who is the target audience for this solution?
The target audience includes all users of the biller portal, with a specific focus on ensuring that customer interactions are secure and compliant with PCI DSS 4.0 standards.
What are the costs associated with this solution?
Development time.
What are the primary features of this solution?
Two or more forms of user verification (e.g., password, mobile verification, etc).
Integration with existing biller portal login system.
User-friendly interface for setup and authentication.
Secure and encrypted verification process.
Support for backup authentication methods in case of primary method failure.
Compliance with PCI DSS 4.0 standards.
Are there revenue or cost savings opportunities for this?
YES (Implementing MFA can potentially reduce the risk of financial losses due to data breaches and non-compliance penalties.)
Does this solution alter our competitive positioning?
YES (This solution will enhance our competitive positioning by showcasing our commitment to security and compliance, which is increasingly important for customers in choosing service providers.)
How do we measure 
success
 on this project?
A reduction in unauthorized access incidents.
User feedback on the MFA system.
Seamless integration with minimal disruption to user experience.
Successful passing of the PCI DSS audit in 2025.
Compliance with PCI DSS 4.0 standards.

Season Deactivation
What is the problem that needs solving?
Currently, there is no mechanism to disable/enable OBD (Online Bank Direct) services temporarily for our billers. This is particularly necessary for billers in Michigan who need to deactivate certain invoice types, mostly tax, during the winter.
What is the preferred solution to this problem?
Implement a system where Invoice Cloud can send notifications to Fiserv and RPPS about the billers and the dates during which OBD payments should not be processed. Instead, customers should send a paper check directly to the biller during these times.
Who is the target audience for this solution?
The primary target audience is our OBD billers, particularly those in Michigan who require seasonal deactivation of specific invoice types.
What are the costs associated with this solution?
Development time, potential costs for Fiserv and MC_RPPS work on their ends.
What are the primary features of this solution?
Ability for IC to automatically inform Fiserv and RPPS about seasonal deactivation of OBD services based on a seasonal deactivation configuration page in the CRM.
Are there revenue or cost savings opportunities for this?
YES (Implementation may reduce operational inefficiencies and enhance customer satisfaction, potentially leading to revenue opportunities.)
Does this solution alter our competitive positioning?
YES (By offering a more flexible and tailored service, it could provide a competitive edge in the market.)
How do we measure success on this project?
Success can be measured by the effective implementation of the seasonal deactivation feature, reduction in incorrect electronic payments during the deactivation period, positive feedback from billers, and overall smooth operation of the system during seasonal changes.
