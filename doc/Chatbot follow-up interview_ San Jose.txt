Call brief
Jill, Ryan, Angela and Dennis from Invoice Cloud, along with Jon, San and Stephanie from SJWG San Jose Water Company, discussed the development of an AI chatbot to handle billing-related inquiries, focusing on the need to prioritize billing support functionality over payment processing capabilities. The participants also explored the potential integration of the chatbot's transcript functionality with the company's CIS system, as well as the challenges of implementing the solution given the company's busy technology agenda and resource constraints. Additionally, the group discussed the company's efforts to transition registered users to paperless billing and the upcoming launch of the customer portal.
Key points
Dennis seeks feedback on chatbot functionality for payments, auto-pay, and paperless billing to enhance customer experience.
Angela demonstrates the chatbot's payment flow, including account authentication, payment amount entry, and credit card processing.
The chatbot has fallback responses if it cannot understand user input, and the experience is designed to be responsive across devices.
The chatbot is currently a guest experience, with plans to add registered user functionality in the future.
Stephanie and Jon indicate that billing-related inquiries, especially about high bills, are a significant source of customer calls that the chatbot could potentially help address.
The team discusses the potential for the chatbot to build a knowledge base that customers can access, and the ability to integrate with the client's CIS system.
The clients express concerns about customer privacy and the need for cybersecurity review, as well as the challenges of integrating the chatbot with their ongoing technology projects.
