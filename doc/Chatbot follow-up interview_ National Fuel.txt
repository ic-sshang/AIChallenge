Call brief
Angela, Minn, Matt Radel and Dennis from Invoice Cloud, along with Jordan, Matt Stoops, Karen, Ashleigh, Amanda, Nathan and Andrea, discussed the use of chatbots for customer support and payment processing. The participants shared their experiences with chatbots, highlighting both the benefits and limitations, and provided feedback on the design and functionality of a proposed chatbot integration, including the need for seamless escalation to live agents and the importance of data analytics to measure the chatbot's effectiveness. The group agreed to schedule a follow-up meeting to further discuss specific scenarios and gather additional input from key stakeholders.
Key points
The goal is to create a chatbot to assist customers with tasks like payments, auto-pay, and paperless billing.
Participants have mixed experiences with chatbots - some find them helpful, others frustrated by lack of relevance.
The prototype demonstrates a payment flow, with options to pay full, minimum, or custom amounts.
The chatbot handles account authentication, payment method selection, and receipt delivery.
Participants note some issues with the prototype, such as incorrect date validation.
The chatbot has limited functionality in this prototype, focused on payments, but plans to expand to other common customer inquiries.
Participants request detailed analytics on chatbot usage, including interaction types and escalations to live agents.
The chatbot will be embedded on the company's website, not hosted externally, allowing for customization.
The team plans to follow up with participants to gather more feedback on common customer scenarios and needs.
Overall, the participants provide constructive feedback to help shape the chatbot's design and functionality.
