Call brief
Matthew, Angela, Ryan and Bill from Invoice Cloud, along with Phil from Mishawaka Utility, discussed a prototype focused on keeping payers on the Mishawaka website and providing them with the ability to make payments, sign up for auto-pay, and access paperless billing. The group explored the potential for the prototype to leverage stored payment methods, integrate an AI chatbot to assist customers, and expand the functionality beyond the initial payment workflow. Phil expressed interest in being a beta tester for the new products and provided valuable feedback on the prototype, highlighting the potential for the chatbot to integrate with Mishawaka's existing systems and the importance of supporting various digital payment methods.
Key points
Mishawaka Utility is exploring AI and chat capabilities to improve customer experience and reduce contact center inquiries.
The prototype demonstrated allows customers to pay bills, sign up for autopay, and access other services through a conversational chat interface.
Mishawaka is open to implementing the chat tool on their website and is interested in the potential to expand its functionality over time.
Mishawaka does not have any policies restricting the use of AI, but will need to review it for legal and cybersecurity compliance.
Mishawaka sees potential for the chat tool to be used by their own employees to assist customers and perform certain tasks more efficiently.
Key success metrics for Mishawaka would include usage metrics, successful transactions, and customer feedback.
There is interest in exploring integrating the chat tool with Mishawaka's existing Dynatouch kiosks to provide interactive support.
Invoice Cloud will continue to collaborate with Mishawaka to gather feedback and iterate on the chat tool's capabilities.
The chat tool should support a variety of payment methods beyond just credit cards, such as ACH, e-wallets, etc.
Mishawaka is interested in participating in Invoice Cloud's biller research panel to provide ongoing feedback.
