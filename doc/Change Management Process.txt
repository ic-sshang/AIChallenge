Change Log
Summary
The Change Management Process and Master Change Log are essential tools designed to systematically capture and document all modifications made to process documents and playbooks. 
To ensure a professional and effective Change Management Process, follow the refined approach. Following this structured approach will help maintain the integrity and consistency of organizational procedures.
Change Management Process
Identify the Change
:
Determine if the change is a process change, document change, or article change.
Clearly define the scope and impact of the change.
Document the Change
:
Record all details of the proposed document or article change.
Include information such as the reason for the change, the expected benefits, and any potential risks.
Submit a CAPA Ticket
:
Use the Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA) process to submit a ticket for the proposed process change.
Ensure the ticket includes all relevant details and supporting documentation of process change.
Review and Approval
:
Conduct a thorough review of the proposed change with relevant stakeholders and subject matter experts.
Ensure all changes are approved by the appropriate authority before implementation.
Implementation
:
Implement the approved changes in a controlled manner.
Ensure all affected parties are informed and trained on the new procedures.
Monitoring and Evaluation
:
Monitor the implementation of the change to ensure it achieves the desired outcomes.
Evaluate the effectiveness of the change and make any necessary adjustments.
Monthly Review Meeting
Agenda
:
Review all changes that have been documented or have a CAPA ticket.
Discuss the impact and effectiveness of recently implemented changes.
Identify any new changes that need to be addressed.
Participants
:
Team Lead
Subject matter experts
Process owners
Outcomes
:
Approval or rejection of proposed changes.
Action items for implementing approved changes.
Updates to the Master Change Log.
The CAPA process, will ensure that all process changes are systematically tracked and managed.
