 Business Logic
Business Logic (click to expand)
Overview
The Biller Portal enhancement involves updates to the business logic to support new functionalities and improve existing ones. These updates are focused on providing a more coherent and comprehensive view of customer activities, invoice histories, and payment lifecycles. The key aspects include:
Enhanced Data Tracking and Storage:
Capture a broader range of customer activities, ensuring no significant event is missed.
Display historical data in a structured manner that allows for easy retrieval and a chronological presentation.
Improved History Tab Functionality:
Differentiate and display invoice history and account history in a clear, non-overlapping manner.
Organize events in a timeline format, providing a complete story of invoices, accounts, and payments.
Segregation of Invoice and Account Histories:
Categorize events accurately into invoice-related or account-related activities.
Ensure overlapping events like email notifications are logically displayed in both histories where relevant, without redundancy.
User-Driven Data Presentation:
Allow users to choose filters in the History tab, enabling them to access the most relevant information quickly.
By implementing these changes in the business logic, the Biller Portal will provide a more efficient, user-friendly, and comprehensive system for billers and support team members to track and understand customer activities, invoice histories, and payment lifecycles.
 Problem Alignment
Problem Alignment (click to expand)
Biller portal users and our support team are face significant challenges in tracking and understanding customer activities. These challenges are primarily due to the limitations of the existing Customer Activity and Email History tabs. Billers have expressed the need for more information, particularly email history and account activity that isn‚Äôt available today. This inadequacy in tracking and understanding customer activities is leading to several issues:
Loss of Important Information:
 Critical customer activity information is being lost, particularly when email addresses are removed from an account. This loss of information impedes the billers' ability to effectively address customer concerns and manage their accounts.
Customer Disputes:
 The lack of comprehensive and accessible records of customer activities contributes to an increased likelihood of disputes with customers. These disputes often arise from misunderstandings or lack of clarity regarding past interactions and transactions.
Support Overload:
 The existing system's limitations have led to an increased number of support tickets. These tickets are primarily related to issues that could have been avoided with better tracking and retention of customer activity records.
Dependence on Data Services:
 Currently, support frequently relies on Data Services for assistance because they are unable to find or access necessary information within the biller portal or CRM. This reliance on DS not only strains resources but also indicates a gap in the system's capability to provide important information to support team members.
¬†Objective
Objective (click to expand)
The primary objective of this project is to enhance the Biller Portal's functionality to better meet the needs of biller portal users and our support team. This includes:
Enhanced Record Retention:
 Improving the system to more effectively retain customer activity history, particularly for email address changes and for account registration and unregistering.
Comprehensive Email History Tab:
 Updating the Email History tab to include a broader range of email types, ensuring all communications sent to customers are documented and easily accessible.
Additional Activity Tracking:
 Include more types of biller activities into the Customer Profile, including Autopay Decline and ACH Reject emails, as well as various payment method and account change notifications.
Reduced Support Tickets and DS Dependency:
 By providing a more comprehensive and accurate data representation within the Biller Portal, the project aims to decrease the number of support tickets related to customer activity tracking and reduce the reliance on Data Services. The reduction in support tickets and DS requests, as well as the reduction in resolution time, will be key metrics for assessing the project's success.
Implementation of Key Features and Flows:
 Implementing key features and flows, such as preserving historical email addresses, extending the "Activity" field's character limit, and providing a detailed log for Payment Method changes.
Overall, this project aims to address the current challenges faced by billers in tracking customer activities, which will enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of the Biller Portal.

User Interaction and Design (click to expand)
Wireframe/mockup
UI Approach
The UI approach will be centered on user-friendliness, with a special focus on the presentation and organization of data to provide a coherent and comprehensive narrative of customer activities. The approach includes:
User-Centric Design:
 Tailoring the interface to the end-user‚Äôs needs, ensuring intuitive navigation and interaction. This involves a deep understanding of how billers and support team use the portal in their daily activities.
Enhanced Data Representation:
 Introducing improvements to the History tab. This includes adding more events and details to provide a complete chronological story of customer and biller interactions, specifically focusing on invoices and associated actions.
Clear Segregation of Information:
 Developing a distinct yet interconnected presentation of invoice history and account history. This will address the current overlap issues, especially for events like email notifications that pertain to both categories.
Interactive and Responsive Elements:
 Implementing interactive elements to allow users to easily navigate between invoice and account history.
User Assistance and Feedback Tools:
 Incorporating tooltips, guides, and contextual help for new features, and gathering user feedback for continuous improvement.
Deliverables
Wireframes and Mockups:
 Including detailed layouts for the revised History tab, showcasing the new structure for invoice and account history.
User Journey Maps:
 Illustrating how users will interact with the updated History tab, highlighting the flow from general overview to detailed invoice/account history.
Interactive Prototypes:
 Simulating the new History tab, allowing for feedback specifically on the improved data organization and presentation.
User Testing and Feedback Compilation:
 Gathering and summarizing feedback from user testing, focusing on the effectiveness of the new History tab in providing a clear and chronological story of customer interactions.
Strategy for Organizing Overlapping Data:
 A specific plan for handling overlapping data between invoice history and account history, ensuring clarity and coherence in the presentation of information.
These deliverables will ensure the Biller Portal‚Äôs History tab is not only functional and informative but also provides a clear and chronological narrative of customer and invoice activities.
