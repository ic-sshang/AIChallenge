 Problem Alignment
Our American Express contract will expire in June 2024. Amex has notified us that they will no longer be working directly with billers to support Amex processing. Going forward, we will need to either move billers to OptBlue or transition to their aggregator model.  The organization has decided that we will transition all Chase billers to the OptBlue platform, and the Braintree billers will be migrated to the aggregator model. This project will focus on the migration of billers/onboarding of new billers to the Optblue platform. 
Our legacy Amex program will be going away. Therefore, billers will need to be migrated to another platform to be able to continue accepting Amex transactions. 
There will be 2 main components of this program:
Migrating existing billers to the Optblue platform
Onboarding new billers to the new Optblue platform
Billers will be migrated in chunks similar to our OBD migration. We expect to onboard/migrate 20-50 billers prior to focusing on larger segments for migration. Most billers typically reconcile at month end. To minimize reconciliation concerns, we will focus on migrating large chunks of billers at the end of the month. We will need to migrate our beta billers within the month so we can quickly identify and address any unexpected issues.  
This migration will be a welcome change to billers. Their Amex funding will now be included with their credit card & ACH deposits from Chase. 
Amex has extended our migration requirements to the end of 2025. As a reminder, here is some data related to our current Amex processing volume.

¬†Objective
The objective of this project is to build out the ability to support the OptBlue model and migrate billers who be staying on Chase to this platform. We will need to identify which billers should be part of the migration to OptBlue and which billers will be migrating to the aggregator model. There will also be a heavy lift to the DB team. Currently reporting is pulled directly from Amex. Once billers are migrated, their Amex reporting data will be included in the existing Chase reporting. A large part of this project will be reviewing the reporting and ensuring that we extract the same AmEx data that we receive today from the Chase reports. Since this migration is impacting the funding process, we will need to communicate the benefits of this change to our portfolio. This will also simplify the onboarding process. Billers won‚Äôt need to apply separately through Amex. 
We need to make some changes within the CRM to support this change, so everyone has visibility into which Amex platform the biller is on. Reporting for amex transactions will no longer come from Amex so we will need to make changes to ensure we are extracting that same data from the Chase reports to ensure a smooth migration. We will need to manage the migration schedule along with Finance to ensure our reporting is aligned appropriately. 
Reconciliation is often a primary concern of our billers. This is another step towards improving that issue. Amex transactions will be deposited with the same batch as their other credit card transactions, making reconciliation easier for our billers. We will also be managing the Amex chargebacks in the same way we handle other card brands, again easing the burden off our billers. For new billers, the onboarding experience for accepting Amex transactions will be faster since it will be included with their other cardbrands. 
Our objectives are: 
Make necessary changes internally to support the OptBlue platform 
Update reporting to ensure we are extracting data from the Chase reports to align with what we collect today from Amex
Define list of billers to be migrated to OptBlue, establish a migration plan and successfully migrate those billers

User Research Background 
We did some extensive research to understand the impact. From a coding perspective, we will need to make some changes in the CRM to update the Amex fields. We will want to include an indicator, so users understand which Amex platform billers are using along with the timeframe. Support/implementations/boarding/sales will need details (on which program they are using) so they can support any questions around Amex transactions.  
There will also be changes needed on the reporting. Currently we pull transactional data directly from Amex. Going forward, Amex data will be sent from Chase in their standard reporting, and we will extract that data to provide financial reporting. 
There will be changes needed on the Payment Reconciliation page. Also, on the batch emails that are sent out, changes will need to be made because Amex is broken out separately.
