Summary
The purpose of this document is to outline:
How to manage new add-on requests from a biller that is in active implementation for a first time launch
How to integrate the add-on into the existing new biller project
We will cover 1) the paperwork needed, 2) the right way to channel the add on through the company and 3) the operations best practices for integrating new products into your existing project.
Commonly received add-on requests
EBPP and Non-EBPP requests can both be placed. Most commonly, we see requests for:
Cloud Store
Cloud Pay
EMV readers
Less frequently: IVR, OBD
Step 1: To integrate or not
Note: If you are unsure whether the add-on can be easily incorporated, please speak with your manager.
Step 2: The paperwork
Whether we implement the add-on with the new biller launch or use a phase 2 approach, we still need the correct paperwork. In accordance with our internal quality assurance processes, we cannot put any products or services live without this. For billers that want to go live with an add-on on the same go-live date as their main imp, it is important that we procure paperwork as soon as possible, as turn-around times can vary.
Notify your manager that an add-on request was received that falls outside of the original scope. Add-on requests do 
not 
filter through the sales manager on the original deal.  Sales Ops has a separate team to work these deals.
Gather the following information from your biller:
Frequency of billing (e.g. monthly, quarterly)
Estimated number of bills sent per frequency period
Average invoice amount (estimate)
Maximum invoice amount (estimate)
Submit a new Salesforce case to Biller Support.  
Subject field should reference the specific add-on.
Status = New
Type = Sales
Sub-Type = Add-On
Description field should include as much detail as possible; specifically what the biller is requesting along w/ billing frequency, number of bills per frequency period, average and max invoice amount. Note if there is any hard deadline or urgency.
