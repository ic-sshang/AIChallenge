 Business Logic
Business Logic (click to expand)
Role Management:
Admins can create new roles, edit existing roles (including the permissions tied to them), and delete roles in the biller portal. However, they cannot delete a role that is in use. If they try, they'll see an error message. Each role requires a unique name.
Role Permissions:
When creating or editing a role, admins can add one or more permissions (organized in categories). Check the confluence page for the full list of permissions. Search and filter for permissions is not necessary.
Assigning Roles to Users:
Admins can easily give a role to a user in the biller portal. Users can have only one role at a time If they get a new role, it'll replace the old one.
Extra Permissions for Users:
Admins can give users additional permissions to what their role grants. These extra permissions won't affect their assigned role. When choosing permissions, the role-based ones will be grayed out and can't be changed. Admins will also have the option to save a set of permissions as a new role.
User Interface and Experience:
The User Management submenu will have links to the Manage Users, Manage Roles, and Permission Audit Log pages. The look and feel of the Roles and Permissions feature should match the existing biller portal design. Pop-up notices will let users know when actions are successful (like creating, saving, or deleting roles).
Access and Auditing:
The system will make sure only authorized admins can manage roles and permissions. It'll also keep track of changes made to roles and permissions (such as who made the change, what was changed, and when), including a report that supports filtering and searching.
 Problem Alignment
Problem Alignment (click to expand)
Today, billers struggle to limit and track the actions of their staff in the Biller Portal. They need better control over permissions and some way to track them in order to meet security and auditing requirements, both inside and outside the organization. Currently, they sometimes have to grant or deny permissions too broadly, which can be a liability.
Imagine an admin who wants customer service reps to process only 
debit
 transactions, but not 
credit 
ones. Today it's just one permission, so blocking credit transactions means blocking debit transactions too. Not only do they have to clean up any mistakes, but this could even lead to legal problems in some places.
Here's another example: A city manages tax payments and parking tickets in their system. They want parking attendants to access and act on Parking Ticket invoice types only, without giving them access to Personal Property Tax and Real Estate Tax records. This isn‚Äôt possible today, so the parking attendant has to rely on a portal user to get information or take action. Their only alternative is to give the attendant access to restricted information that they shouldn‚Äôt see.
And lastly, consider how time-consuming it is to manually set permissions for each user (especially when you have 350+ like NFG). It would be much easier to just assign a role with pre-defined permissions one time, then select the users in that role. This would also make it quick and easy to manage any changes in permission that might occur.

 Target Audience
Target Audience (click to expand)
Group
Impact
Biller Administrators
Biller administrators are responsible for managing permissions and access control for their staff in the Biller Portal. Enhancements to permission granularity, role-based access, and streamlined processes will directly impact their ability to effectively manage permissions and ensure compliance with auditing and security requirements.
Customer Service Representatives (CSRs)
CSRs interact with customers and process transactions in the Biller Portal. Improved permission management will ensure that CSRs have appropriate access to perform their tasks (e.g., processing debit transactions) while preventing unauthorized actions (e.g., processing credit transactions). This will enhance operational efficiency and reduce the risk of errors.
Finance and Compliance Teams
Finance and compliance teams within billing organizations oversee financial transactions and compliance with legal and security requirements. Granular permission management and improved auditing capabilities in the Biller Portal will enable these teams to better monitor and ensure compliance, reducing the risk of legal and compliance issues.
Support Team
Support teams handle technical issues and support requests from billers and their staff. A reduction in permission-related support tickets and improved permission management features will allow support teams to allocate their resources more effectively and address other needs of the organization.

 Technical Architecture
Technical Architecture (click to expand)
User Interface (UI) - Permission Management:
This component includes the user interface pages and elements used by admins to configure and manage permissions, roles, and access rights for users.
Permission Management System:
This is the back-end system responsible for storing and managing permission configurations, user roles, and access rights. It interacts with the UI and the Access Control Layer.
Access Control Layer:
The Access Control Layer is responsible for enforcing permissions and access rights based on user roles. It ensures that users only have access to features, data, and actions they are authorized to perform.
Audit Logging and Reporting:
This component captures and tracks permission changes and user actions for auditing purposes. It generates logs and reports that can be reviewed and exported by admins.
User Onboarding Workflow:
The User Onboarding Workflow is the process through which new users are added to the Biller Portal. It includes the assignment of pre-defined roles and permissions during onboarding.
Data Store:
The Data Store is the database where information about permissions, roles, users, and audit logs is stored.
 Data Flow
Data Flow (click to expand)
When an admin configures permissions or roles through the UI, the changes are sent to the Permission Management System.
The Permission Management System processes the changes and updates the Data Store accordingly.
When a user logs in and attempts to access a feature or perform an action, the Access Control Layer verifies the user's permissions and role against the configurations stored in the Data Store.
If the user is authorized, access is granted, and the user's action is logged by the Audit Logging and Reporting component.
If the user is not authorized, access is denied, and the user‚Äôs action is logged by the Audit Logging and Reporting component.

¬†Assumptions
Assumptions (click to expand)
Users
Admins in billing organizations are expected to have a basic grasp of permissions and roles, as well as experience managing access rights for their staff.
Biller Portal users, such as Customer Service Representatives and Parking Attendants, might have different job duties that call for varying levels of access to the portal's features and data.
User satisfaction and retention are influenced by the permission management system's ease of use, efficiency, and security.
Technical Constraints
The current architecture and database structure of the Biller Portal can handle the updates needed for the granular permission system and role-based access control.
The development and testing environments are equipped with the necessary tools and resources to support the implementation and testing of the new permission management features.
System performance and scalability shouldn't be significantly affected by the new permission management system.
Business Goals
Enhancing the permission management system is in line with the business goals of boosting security, compliance, and user satisfaction in the Biller Portal.
Streamlining user onboarding and reducing support tickets related to permission issues are crucial for improving operational efficiency.
Offering granular permissions and role-based access control could give us a competitive edge and attract more billers to the portal.
Legal and Compliance Considerations
The revamped permission management system should adhere to all relevant legal, regulatory, and industry standards for data privacy and security.
Providing audit logs and reports for permission changes and user actions is essential for meeting auditing requirements.¬†
