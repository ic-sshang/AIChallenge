Responding to Post-Implementation Survey
Description
Process for how we respond internally to negative post-implementations survey responses.

Scope
The purpose of this SOP is to provide a structured approach for addressing negative feedback from customers on post-implementation surveys. It aims to ensure consistency, professionalism, and prompt resolution of issues raised, ultimately improving customer satisfaction and fostering long-term relationships.
This SOP applies to all employees and teams responsible for responding to and managing customer feedback after an implementation project, including Customer Success Managers, Support Teams, and Account Managers.
Background
The post-implementation survey gets sent to billers 
2 weeks
 after they go live.  It will get sent to each contact in Salesforce that has the “Implementations/Project Manager” checkbox checked.
Its important to remind the biller to fill out the survey when they receive it.  The survey can be found here:
https://www.surveymonkey.com/r/Post_Implementation_Feedback
 
Process
Definitions
Negative Feedback
: Feedback indicating dissatisfaction with the implementation process, product, marketing, or service. This can include low ratings, complaints, or specific issues raised by the customer.
Post-Implementation Survey
: A survey sent to customers two weeks after they went live to gather feedback on the experience.
Key Responsibilities
Transition Success Management Team
: Acknowledge feedback, schedule follow-up calls, and communicate with customers.
Implementations Team
: Investigate and resolve technical issues, assist with corrective actions.
Marketing Team
: Address Marketing or product-related issues.
Management
: Approve escalations and handle sensitive customer relations.
Quality Assurance
Responses should always maintain a high level of professionalism and empathy.
Timelines should be adhered to in order to maintain customer trust.
Corrective actions should aim to prevent recurrence of similar issues.
Roles
Lead Person (LP)
 - 
The role of LP referenced below will be the Transition Success Manager (TSM) for billers > $3K MRR, and Director of Implementations (DOI) for billers < $3K MRR.
Director of Implementations (DOI)
Marketing Specialist (MS)
Procedure
1. 
Acknowledge the Feedback (Within 1-3 Business Day)
Step 1
: Monitor survey results regularly to identify negative feedback.  Negative feedback would be any question that receives (Strongly Disagree, Disagree, Not very helpful, Not helpful at all).
Step 2
: The 
LP
 will Acknowledge receipt of the feedback through email.
Example template for response: 
Dear [Customer Name],

Thank you for taking the time to provide feedback on our recent implementation. We sincerely apologize that your experience did not meet expectations. Your feedback is extremely valuable to us, and we are committed to addressing your concerns as quickly and effectively as possible.

We will review the details and reach out to you soon to discuss further.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Title]
Ensure the tone is empathetic, professional, and solution-oriented.
2. 
Investigate the Issue (Within 5 Business Days)
The 
LP
 will triage biller survey feedback and review each of the issues reported in the response.  
Step 1
: Review the post-implementation survey, including any detailed comments and ratings.
Step 2
: Communicate internally with relevant teams (Implementations, Support, Marketing) to gather insights or understand what might have gone wrong.
Step 3
: Collect and document the findings, including potential causes and suggested actions to resolve the issue.
Step 4:
  Log CAPAs where necessary.  
CAPA - Continuous Improvement Program Overview
 
3. 
Schedule an internal Call to review feedback and coordinate response (Within 5-10 Business Days).
Step 1
: Send out a summary of issues to internal team DOI & MS.
Step 2
: Schedule internal call with DOI if feedback is implementation related, MS if feedback is Marketing related, or both if feedback is implementations and marketing related.
4. 
LP to
 
schedule a call with the Customer or attend an upcoming Transition Success Management call if MRR > $3K (Within 5-10 Business Days)
Step 1
: If MRR > $3K contact customer’s TSM to determine which upcoming TSM call you should attend to review survey feedback ELSE Contact the customer to schedule a call to discuss the feedback further.
Step 2
: During the call, express genuine concern and appreciation for the customer’s candidness. Focus on understanding the root cause of their dissatisfaction.
Step 3
: Share findings from the internal investigation and offer potential solutions or next steps to resolve the issue.
Step 4
: Provide a clear timeline for any further actions that need to be taken.
4. 
Implement Corrective Actions (Within 10-21 Business Days)
Step 1
: Execute any agreed-upon corrective actions. This could involve:
Modifying or optimizing the solution.
Providing additional training or support.
Offering compensation or goodwill gestures (if appropriate).  For example, sending a few pizzas can go a long way!
Step 2
: Ensure all actions are documented and tracked.
5. 
LP to
 
Follow-Up Communication (Within 3 Weeks of Initial Contact)
Step 1
: Follow up with the customer within two weeks to ensure their concerns have been fully addressed.
Example template for response:
Dear [Customer Name],

I hope this message finds you well. I wanted to follow up on our recent discussion regarding your feedback on the implementation. I trust that the actions we’ve taken have resolved the concerns you raised, but please do let us know if there is anything else we can assist with.

Your satisfaction is our priority, and we deeply value your partnership.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Title]
6. 
LP To Escalate if Necessary
Step 1
: If the customer remains dissatisfied, escalate the issue to senior management or the appropriate escalation team for further resolution.
Step 2
: Keep the customer informed about the escalation and provide updates throughout the process.
7. 
Document the Process
Step 1
: Log the feedback, actions taken, and final resolution in the customer relationship management (CRM) system or feedback tracking tool.  HOW SHOULD WE LOG RESULTS?  
Joint Impact Plan?
Step 2
: Identify any patterns or recurring issues from negative feedback to inform product or service improvements.
8. 
Continuous Improvement
Step 1
: Conduct internal reviews of negative feedback trends quarterly or after major implementations.
Step 2
: Share insights with relevant teams to continuously improve product offerings, services, and processes.
KPIs for Success
Reduction in negative post-implementation feedback over time.
Improved customer satisfaction scores (CSAT/NPS) post-response.
Timely resolution of customer complaints.
