 Rollout Plan
MVP - Initial timeline was for RPPS to be completed by September 30, 2022. Team has been marching to this date and was prepared to start transitioning early access billers. On 9/12/22, MasterCard informed the test plan they recommended will require an additional 3-4 months of work on their end. We will be continuing to work towards the previous timeline to complete the development work. 
User stories are prioritized based on level of importance first. 
We are planning to run production testing with 1-2 smaller billers once development is complete. 
Testing/Migration Plan:
Our test plan has shifted to focus on billers who are only using Fiserv for OBD payments. We will begin a pilot program for Fiserv early access users to begin using the system and proving feedback. We will provide launch support for these billers and work internally for any additional development updates which will be needed to support the overall OBD launch.

Communication
Marketing has put together a formal communication plan to include the upcoming changes and to highlight actions needed by the biller. Communication will include providing information in the Cloud Burst, sending emails to billers as well as hosting a formal webinar highlighting the upcoming changes and rollout plan so billers are aware. We will also be using Pendo as a way to provide updates and reminders to billers. There will be changes to the existing process that will need to be communicated clearly internally and to billers including the following:
New originator ID - billers will need to notify their bank of this new number
matched based funding
consolidated reporting 
New feature to support funding at the invoice type level. 
OBD batches will close at the same time as their credit card batch so depending on whether their standard batch close time is after 5pm EST, funding will be 1 day later. 
Successful match close process is now tied to funding 
At transition, all open unmatched items will be matched and funded to create a clean transition
