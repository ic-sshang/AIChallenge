Runbook name
Free Time Off Policy
Description
Required process for logging Free Time Off when you’re out of the office.  Policy only applies to time off requests that are a day or more in duration and doesn't include company holidays or summer Friday off periods.

Summary
After your time off request is approved in workday, you’ll need to update the Implementations public calendar.  Recording your time off in a public calendar is important as it allows everyone to be aware of colleagues' availability, helping to avoid scheduling conflicts and ensuring that necessary coverage is maintained. 
Process
Steps
Description
Role Responsible
1
Submit your time off in Workday
Wait until you receive approval in Workday from your manager for your time off request.
Employee
2
Log time off in public Implementations calendar
After you receive approval from your manager on your time off request, log the request in the Implementations' team public calendar.  In Outlook, click Calendars → “IC-Implementations” calendar and record your time off.  Make sure you include all days you’re off. 
Employee
3
Notify your project contacts 
Notify the contacts for the projects you're working on that you’ll be out of the office, and who they should contact for questions, comments, or escalations.
Employee
4
Set Automatic replies in Outlook
Set your Auto replies (out of office) reminders in outlook.  In Outlook, File → Automatic Replies.
